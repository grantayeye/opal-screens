I posted the other day about AI getting 95% right and why that's actually dangerous. A few people pushed back saying I was being too hard on it.

I want to be clear — I'm not anti-AI. I use it every day. It saves me real time on real work and I think if you're running a business without it at this point, you're leaving money on the table.

But here's what I've noticed after months of using it heavily: AI never tells you when it's guessing. It doesn't pause and say "I'm not confident about this part." It gives you a wrong answer with the exact same tone as a right one. No hesitation, no asterisk, nothing.

Think about how you'd handle a new employee who did that. Someone who presented everything like they were 100% sure, even when they were making it up. You'd stop trusting them pretty fast.

That's how I've learned to treat AI. Like a really talented new hire who hasn't earned unsupervised work yet. I give it tasks, I review the output, and I never let it run without a check.

The people getting the most out of AI right now aren't the ones trusting it blindly. They're the ones who know exactly where it's strong and where it falls apart.

I'm still figuring out that line. But I'm paying attention, and that's the part that matters.
